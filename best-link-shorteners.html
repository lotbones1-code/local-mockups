#2

Bitly

Free / $10-$199/mo

Bitly is the most recognized link shortener, operating since 2008. It has deep enterprise integrations and brand recognition. However, in 2025 Bitly added interstitial ads on all free links and gutted the free plan to just 10 links/month. Custom domains require the $29/mo Growth plan, and features like geo targeting cost $199/mo on Premium. It's still reliable, but you're paying a premium for the brand name.

Pros

  • Most widely recognized brand
  • Deep enterprise integrations
  • Extensive documentation
  • Reliable uptime and infrastructure

Cons

  • Free links show interstitial ads
  • Only 10 links/mo on free plan
  • Custom domains require $29/mo plan
  • Geo targeting costs $199/mo
  • Closed source, no self-host option
  • No conversion tracking or A/B testing
Best for: Large enterprises already invested in the Bitly ecosystem who need maximum third-party integrations.
#3

Short.io

Free / $19/mo

Short.io offers a generous free plan with up to 1,000 branded short links and 5 custom domains. Analytics include click tracking, geographic data, device types, and referral sources. The paid plans start at $19/mo. It's a solid mid-range option for teams that need branded links without the complexity of a full attribution platform.

Pros

  • 1,000 links on free plan
  • 5 custom domains free
  • Detailed click analytics
  • API access on free plan

Cons

  • No conversion tracking
  • No A/B testing
  • Smaller community than Dub.co or Bitly
Best for: Small businesses and marketers who need lots of branded links on a budget.
#4

Rebrandly

Free / $13/mo

Rebrandly focuses specifically on branded links and custom domains. The free plan includes 10 links/month and 1 custom domain. Paid plans start at $13/mo. It has a clean dashboard and solid link management features, though it lacks the developer tools and advanced analytics that Dub.co offers.

Pros

  • Strong branding focus
  • 1 custom domain on free plan
  • Clean, intuitive dashboard
  • Affordable paid plans

Cons

  • Only 10 links/mo free
  • Limited API on free plan
  • No open-source option
  • Basic analytics compared to Dub.co
Best for: Brands focused primarily on link branding and custom domain aesthetics.
#5

TinyURL

Free / $12.99/mo

TinyURL is one of the oldest URL shorteners (since 2002). The free plan offers unlimited link creation with no signup required — just paste a URL and get a short link. However, free links have no analytics, no custom domains, and no API. Paid plans at $12.99/mo add analytics and branded domains. It's best for quick, one-off shortening when you don't need tracking.

Pros

  • Unlimited free links
  • No signup required
  • Instant, no-friction shortening
  • Longest track record (since 2002)

Cons

  • No analytics on free plan
  • No custom domains free
  • No API on free plan
  • No advanced features (A/B, targeting)
Best for: Quick, one-off link shortening when you just need a shorter URL with zero setup.
#6

BL.INK

Free / $48/mo

BL.INK is an enterprise-focused link management platform with strong analytics and compliance features. The free plan includes 1,000 links and 1 custom domain. Paid plans start at $48/mo, making it one of the more expensive options. It's well-suited for regulated industries and large teams that need audit trails and compliance reporting.

Pros

  • 1,000 links on free plan

  • SOC 2 compliant

Cons

  • Expensive ($48/mo starting)
  • Overkill for small teams
  • Not open source
Best for: Enterprise teams in regulated industries needing compliance and audit features.
#7

Cutt.ly

Free / $25/mo

Cutt.ly offers unlimited free link shortening with basic click analytics, making it a solid choice for casual users. The free plan includes API access and basic stats. Paid plans at $25/mo add custom domains, team features, and advanced analytics. It's straightforward and functional, but lacks the developer tools and depth of platforms like Dub.co.

Pros

  • Simple, easy to use

Cons
